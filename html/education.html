My Education Timeline

Einstein determined that time is relative. I don't know why. But time really flies so fast.

  • Manuel S. Rojas Elementary School

    I attended Manuel S. Rojas Elementary School from the time I was 5 years old until I graduated. I attended school there for seven years, from kindergarten through sixth grade. Since the school is close to our home, my parents decided to enroll me in MRES. I barely remember my experiences at this school, so please forgive me if I am unable to tell you a story.

  • Cavite National High School

    My high school life was not that different from others'. I met AJ, JM, and JD when we were in seventh grade. We were classmates under the Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) curriculum. At this part of my life, I learned many things, even those that were not really worth learning for. I completed junior high school and moved up with them.

  • Cavite National High School — Senior High School

    Senior high school means new classmates, new teachers—almost everything is new. I took the Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) strand. Unfortunately, I and my friends were not classmates. I was in STEM Section C, and they were in Sections A and B. This part has been very hard and challenging for me. Apart from not studying well, I became addicted to online games. But luckily, I survived senior high school and graduated with a medal.

  • La Verdad Christian College

    I started my college life here at La Verdad Christian College - Apalit. I am currently taking the Associate in Computer Technology program. Apart from being a nice school, instructors are very considerate and students are disciplined. We have a set of rules and biblical moral principles. I am happy to be a student at LVCC because I know that This school does everything possible to help students succeed. to excel academically and to be a God-fearing individual.
